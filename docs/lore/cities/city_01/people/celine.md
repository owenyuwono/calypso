# Celine

## Basics
- **Role**: Potion seller
- **Age**: Late 20s
- **Personality**: Practical, warm but business-minded. Proud of her craft. Direct — doesn't waste words.
- **Appearance**: Simple travelling clothes, apron stained with alchemical residue, hair tied back. Carries a satchel of dried herbs.

## Backstory

Celine grew up in one of the coastal towns to the east, where her mother ran an apothecary. She learned herb identification and basic alchemy as a child — not from formal training, but from helping her mother grind ingredients and mix tinctures every day after school.

When she was nineteen, her mother's shop burned in an accident. A mislabelled solvent, a candle left too close. The building was gone in an hour. Nobody was hurt, but the family lost everything — stock, equipment, recipes. Her mother never recovered the will to start over.

Celine came to the city three years ago with a small stock of potions and a determination to rebuild what her mother couldn't. She chose this city over larger towns because rent was cheaper and adventurers — her ideal customers — were plentiful. She set up near the fountain because foot traffic is highest there.

She's done well. Not wealthy, but stable. She makes her own potions using slime jelly (the key ingredient) and herbs she gathers outside the walls in the early morning before the market opens. She's one of the few non-adventurers who regularly goes outside the walls — she just knows where the herbs grow and how to avoid the slimes.

## Motivations
- Build a proper shop with a roof (she works from a stall right now)
- Find a reliable supplier for slime jelly so she doesn't have to buy from adventurers at their markup
- Eventually open a real apothecary, like her mother's

## Fears
- Fire (obviously)
- Losing her stock to theft or spoilage
- Being undercut by a wealthier merchant with more capital

## Relationships
- Respects the forge family — they were here first, they earned their place
- Mild tension with adventurers who sell her jelly at inflated prices (she needs it, they know it)
- Friendly with the temple healer — they serve similar needs but don't compete (he handles injuries, she handles prevention)
- Doesn't trust the noble quarter merchants (they have the capital to crush her if they wanted to)

## Dialogue Notes
- Direct, not flowery. "What do you need?" not "Welcome, weary traveler!"
- Mentions her potions' quality — she's proud of her work
- Will complain about jelly prices if you ask about her day
- Gets warmer with repeat customers

## Quests
- **Jelly Supply**: Bring her 5 slime jelly — she'll give you a discount on potions or a free batch
- **Future**: Help her find a rare herb in a new zone; help her deal with a merchant trying to undercut her
